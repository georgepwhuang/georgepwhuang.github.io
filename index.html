About me

I am Po-Wei (George) Huang, a research assistant at the Computer Science Group of the Centre for Quantum Technologies. I graduated from the National University of Singapore with a B.Comp. in Computer Science, where I specialized in algorithms and artificial intelligence. I am currently supervised by PI Prof. Patrick Rebentrost, who also supervised my bachelor's thesis on post-variational quantum neural networks with Prof. Rahul Jain.

I am generally interested in quantum algorithms, quantum learning theory, and quantum complexity theory. My current research directions include:

  • quantum machine learning with provable guarantees
  • quantum algorithms for optimization
  • quantum solutions for algorithmic game theory and physical applications

I have previously worked with Prof. Min-Yen Kan at the Web Information Retrieval / Natural Language Processing Group (WING) on supervised and semi-supervised strategies for contextual document structure extraction with medium-sized transformer language models.
